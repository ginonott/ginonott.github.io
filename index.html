HackerWave - Retrofitted Smart Microwave

HackerWave was a group project built during a 48 hour hackathon at Cornell. Our idea was to take a microwave and embed a RaspberryPI so that we could scan a barcode on a pre-made meal such as hot pockets and have the microwave know exactly how to cook it perfectly.

The project consisted of a flask server that hosted an HTTP service, an android app that would read a barcode and send the barcode information to the microwave, and the code that would interact with the circuitry of the microwave via GPIO pins. To make it more difficult and comical, the microwave was from the 1980s and had no wiring diagrams or schematics. I helped decode the voltage signals needed to program the microwave. We used the RaspberryPI to plug into the same circuits that the number pad plugged into. We measured the voltages on the circuit board as buttons were pressed to figure out what signals we had to mimic. Once we had compiled all the possible values, we built a python program to output signals via the gpio pins and a circuit to convert the low voltage power of the RaspberryPI into the high voltage power used by the microwave circuit board. By the end of the hackathon, we had an android app that could scan barcodes, send them to the HTTP server, translate that to the instructions needed to program the microwave, and start it. Unfortunately, we burned out the RaspberryPI due to bad wiring (oops).
